Hello, everyone!

My name is Stephen Mann, and this is the website I have built from scratch. Feel free to cycle through the tabs to navigate each section and learn more about me.

I started this website to teach myself new <coding> techniques as it has always been something I have been interested in learning. To challenge myself, I didn’t utilize any websites (i.e., Wordpress, Wix, Squarespace, etc.) that would do much of the work for me. Development began in a .txt file out of Notepad, then saving-as a .html file, and finally opening that file in a browser to check to see if everything was done properly. As you can imagine, this was quite tedious, so I swapped over to utilizing a text editor program called Atom IDE. It made everything easier with its syntax highlighting and overall layout. After that, everything started to fall into place and development proceeded swiftly.

This website is stored on my GitHub account and fully hosted on my friend's server. If you are having trouble accessing this website, come back in a couple of hours and it should be back up and running. I will be creating my own server once I secure employment, and then all hosting will be done there. If you notice any issues or have any advice on how to improve the coding, please email me to let me know. I'm always open to constructive criticism and adding new content.

I wasn't chasing numbers. The goal was more reliable power without affecting driveability. The engine was also not iced down or anything before the dyno run, so this is what I will be seeing in everyday driving. Just driven over, strapped down, and dyno run performed.

My 2003 Chevy TrailBlazer Custom Audio System

I researched and put together an entirely new audio system for my 2003 TrailBlazer back in my senior year of college. All of the installation was performed by me with the help of my Dad and his vast set of tools while I was home for a weekend. Unfortunately, I didn't take too many pictures throughout the process because it was freezing cold outside. The following components were installed, and the stock system was completely gutted:

  • Head Unit: Clarion CX-501
  • Front Component Speakers: DLS X-SC62
  • Amp for Component Speakers: Boston Acoustics GTA-502
  • Subwoofer: Image Dynamics ID10 D2 V.3 (Sealed Enclosure)
  • Amp for Subwoofer: MTX Thunder500.1

This was my first ever car audio install, and I learned a lot from this project. The DLS speakers sounded fantastic, but they have a relatively low sensitivity and I underestimated how much wind noise the TrailBlazer generated at highway speeds. I was extremely happy that I included an excessive amount of wiring for the subwoofer to allow me to put the enclosure on the front seat if I ever needed to fold down the rear seats to store stuff. This became quite useful in the four times I moved after installation.

My Parent's Home Theater System

I bought my parents a new home theater sound system to go with their new HD TV in 2014. The new HD TV replaced an old rear-projection TV that was older than me at the time whose internal lenses melted and no longer properly combined the RGB colors on screen to make the seamless image. As such, the audio system deserved an upgrade as well.

They were previously utilizing a pair of Bose 901 Series IIIs that my Dad managed to snag from a band who was throwing them out after using them in their concert in the 70s. The issue was that it would have required spending a good deal of money on equipment that could take the outdated 901s and connect them to modern-era TVs. Because of this, I convinced them to upgrade to a modern sound system for around the same price as what it would require doing that, and I offered to pay for the whole thing. It was a "thank you" for letting me live at home and commute to work after college until I paid off my student loan debt and got a new job. The system comprised of the following components:

  • Receiver: Denon AVR-X2000
  • LCR Speakers: Ascend Acoustics CMT-340 SE
  • Subwoofer: SVS SB-1000

Overall, this system came out fantastic! I have nothing but praise for all the components involved, and my parents love it as well. The only thing I would change after-the-fact would be to go with the SVS PB-1000 ported sub instead of the SB-1000 sealed sub due to my parent's living room being completely open to the kitchen, leading to a rather large environment that needs to move a lot of air to hit the extremely low notes with true authority. The PB-1000 is said to reach 10 dB higher than the SB-1000. For those who don't know, that's quite a large jump on the logarithmic scale and will sound around twice as loud at peak volume to the average person!
